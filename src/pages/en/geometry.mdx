    ### 2.3 Graphical definitions of sine, cosine and tangent functions

    Let's consider a circunference with radius of length r with centre at the origin *O* in a rectangular coordinate system, and a point P with coordinates (x,y) in the circunference. And let's name $\alpha$ the angle between the x-axis and the segment OP, as shown in figure below. Then, $\sin \alpha$ is the ratio y/r, `cos alpha` is the ratio x/r and $\tan \alpha$ is the ratio y/x.

    <figure className="centered">
        <img src="/images/Circunference_and_coordinates.svg" width="300" height="300" alt="Diagram of a circle with point P and inscribed right triangle."/>
        <figcaption><b>Figure 2.2</b> Circunference of radius r with a point P and angle $\alpha$ with x-axis.</figcaption>
    </figure>

    Then, we define the sine, cosine and tangent functions as:

    </div>
