2.2 Pythagorean theorem

In any right triangle \(\triangle ABC\), where c is the length of the hypotenuse and a and b are the lengths of the legs,

\(a^2 + b^2 = c^2\)
[2.2]

2.3 Graphical definitions of sine, cosine and tangent functions

Let's consider a circunference with radius of length r with centre at the origin O in a rectangular coordinate system, and a point P with coordinates (x,y) in the circunference. And let's name α the angle between the x-axis and the segment \(\overline {OP}\), as shown in figure below. Then, \(\sin \alpha\) is the ratio y/r, `cos alpha` is the ratio x/r and \(\tan \alpha\) is the ratio y/x.

Then, we define the sine, cosine and tangent functions as:

\(\sin \alpha = y/r\)
[2.2]

\(\cos \alpha = x/r\)
[2.3]

\(\tan \alpha = y/x\)
[2.4]
